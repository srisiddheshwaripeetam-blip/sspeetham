Sri Sivachidananda Bharathi Swamy, originally named Bandhakavi Seetaramanjaneyalu, was born on September 22, 1929 in Tanuku, Andhra Pradesh. He was the son of Sri Venkataramayya and Smt. Subhadramma.\n\nPursued his education in Bheemavaram, Tanuku, and Visakhapatnam, later establishing himself as a distinguished lawyer and master in the field of law. Alongside his professional career, he actively contributed to society through his involvement in organizations such as the Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh (RSS), Viswa Hindu Parishad, Rotary Club, Brahmanaseva sangam and Nannaya Bhattaraka peetam.\n\nSri Bandhakavi Seetaramanjaneyalu was initiated into Sanyasadeeksha by Sri Trivikramaramananda Bharathi Swamy, who bestowed him with the name Sri Sivachidananda Bharathi Swamy. Following the siddhi of his Guru, he ascended as the Third Pontiff of Sri Siddheswari Peetam.\n\nWith his profound expertise in law, Swamiji resolved numerous legal matters concerning the Peetam and its sister organizations. His leadership brought financial stability, enabling the merging of several temples under the Peetam’s administration. Devotees lovingly referred to him as “Abhinava Mounaswamy.”\n\nSwamiji was also a master in Vasthu Shastra and Jyotishya, and during his tenure, he spearheaded the reconstruction of many temples and the establishment of new ones, strengthening the spiritual foundation of the Peetam.\n\nSri Sivachidananda Bharathi Swamy maintained cordial relations with people of diverse religions and practices, embodying inclusivity and harmony. He worked tirelessly for the upliftment of the Brahmin community, organizing meetings and initiatives aimed at their development.\n\nIn the year 2002 swamiji announced Sri Prasadaraya Kulapati, a renowned educationist from Guntur, as his successor and initiated him into Sanyasadeeksha and bestowed upon him the name Sri Siddeshwarananda Bharathi Swamy and attained Mahasamadhi on December 17, 2002.
